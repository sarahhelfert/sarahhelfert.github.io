About Me

I'm a PhD candidate in Applied Mathematics at UNC Charlotte, finishing in December 2026. My dissertation focuses on numerical methods for stochastic differential equations — the mathematics of systems that evolve randomly over time.

Outside my dissertation, I've spent the last three years as a research engineer at Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, building and deploying deep learning architectures for large-scale scientific computing problems.

I'm looking for roles where mathematical rigor and practical engineering are both valued — in ML, quantitative analytics, or applied AI. I'm based in Charlotte, NC and open to hybrid or remote positions.
